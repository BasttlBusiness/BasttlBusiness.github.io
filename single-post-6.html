Used Car Vs New Car

Buying a car is one of the most important dream of everyone. In case of middle class people, it’s a dream to buy own car. Everyone wants to buy a amazing car in their budget. Off course buying a new car is a smart choice, but on the other hand it is economical too. So, it creates a massive confusion among people. They research too much and ask other’s opinion, even after all this, only some people are able to take the right decision.

Insurance Premium for a used car much lower than the new car

One of the most significant advantages of a used car is lower insurance expenses because as vehicle’s age increase, insurance premium decreases. When you sell or buy a car, the premium is computed based on the car’s depreciation. The insurance premium amount is recalculated following the transfer of ownership, resulting in lower car insurance. It would be great deal!

Used cars are the best option for a Learner Driver

If you are a learner driver then used cars are unquestionably the finest option. When you first start driving, you’re likely to get a few scratches or dents, so it’s a good idea to go out and get a new car. You can master the basics of driving and gain enough confidence to tackle India’s congested roads.

Used car are Economically Friendly

From the last two years, people has faced a lot of financial challenges due to this pandemic. And after this, buying a new car will again a big challenge. So what if you get a reliable and luxury car within the less budget. In comparison to a new car, the cost of ownership for a used car is significantly lower. This is primarily due to excessive depreciation, registration fees, high tax rates, RTO and costly car insurance premiums.

New Car hits by high Depreciation Rate

In most cases, a new car loses the majority of its value in the first few years after it is purchased. So, if you buy a brand new car, the depreciation rate is too high in the first three years. Depreciation Rate is the fundamental reason why pre-owned cars are usually less expensive than new cars. The amount of value that a vehicle loses over time is referred to as depreciation. Generally the vehicle have 10% depreciation rate. It means with a year the value of your money which you spent on new car deprecate 10%. If you have a well enough budget then you should go with buying a brand new car. But on the other hand, if you have a limited budget and you want value of your money, then pre-owned cars are the Best and economically friendly choice!
