Maintain Your Car Health During Monsoons

The monsoon season is approaching, and with it comes heavy rains and severe traffic bottlenecks. Long traffic congestion, combined with filth, degrade the car’s general condition. Water tends to accumulate in the steering wheel, brakes, and doors, causing electrical and rust issues. That can harm your car.

• Special care of batteries and electronics

Because water and electricity do not get along, it is critical to inspect all wiring and electronics before starting the car during the monsoon season. All cables must be checked, as must all wire casings, and any water near the car’s electronics must be dried.

• Servicing before monsoon arrival

The first effect of rainwater on cars is Rusting. Car wax can be useful in this situation. Car wax gives a smooth finish of car surface which prevents water from sitting on the car’s body. If the vehicle requires service during the monsoon season, it might be sent ahead of schedule because the service will rule out any other issues that may go unnoticed by the naked eye.

• Drying materials for your car

During the monsoon season, fungal growth and odour in the car are caused by wet shoes and interior. Keeping a newspaper on foot mats because it absorbs water quickly and use and throw material. Keep a dry towel on hand to cover the seats, allowing the towels to absorb the water while keeping the seats dry.

These tips help you to keep your car fresh. So, monsoon don’t irritate you anymore.
